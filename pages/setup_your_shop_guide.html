Space Planning & Design

Optimize your barbershop layout for efficiency, comfort, and profitability

Layout Essentials

1

Minimum Space Requirements

Allow 80-100 sq ft per chair, including circulation space. Factor in waiting area, reception, and storage.

2

Traffic Flow

Create clear pathways between stations. Avoid bottlenecks near the entrance and reception area.

3

Electrical & Plumbing

Plan for adequate outlets at each station. Consider wash basin placement and hot water access.

4

Storage Solutions

Include tool storage, product displays, and cleaning supply areas in your design.

Lighting Design

Proper lighting is crucial for precision work. Combine natural light with LED task lighting at each station.

  • • 500+ lux at cutting stations
  • • Color temperature 4000-5000K
  • • Minimize shadows and glare

Ventilation

Maintain air quality with proper ventilation systems to remove hair particles and product fumes.

  • • 6-8 air changes per hour
  • • Individual station ventilation
  • • HEPA filtration recommended

Accessibility

Ensure your shop is accessible to all clients with proper entrance width and maneuvering space.

  • • 32" minimum door width
  • • 5' turning radius clearance
  • • Accessible parking spaces

Licensing & Legal Requirements

Navigate the legal requirements to operate your barbershop legally and safely

Required Licenses & Permits

Business License

General business operation permit from your city/county

Cost: $50-200

Barbershop License

State-specific barbershop establishment license

Cost: $100-500

Health Department Permit

Sanitation and safety compliance certification

Cost: $75-300

Fire Department Approval

Fire safety and occupancy limits certification

Cost: $50-150

Signage Permit

Exterior signage approval from local authorities

Cost: $25-100

Workers' Compensation

Required if you have employees

Cost: Varies by payroll

Insurance Coverage
